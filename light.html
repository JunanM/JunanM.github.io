Light Pollution

“May it be a light to you in dark places, when all other lights go out.” ― J.R.R. Tolkien

What is Light Pollution?

Light pollution is the "brightening of the night sky caused by street lights and other man-made sources, which has a disruptive effect on natural cycles and inhibits the observation of stars and planets."

The Circadian Rhythm

Light stimulates retinal nerves, which send signals to the suprachiasmatic nucleus. The suprachiasmatic nucleus is "a pair of cel clusters in the hypothalamus that control [circadian, or biological], rhythm." Stimulation of the suprchiasmatic nucleus will cause the pineal gland to reduce its production of a sleep-inducing hormone called "melatonin." (Myers 229). Reduced melatonin-levels will cause one to feel less sleepy, thus staying awake longer. Exposure to extended periods of artificial light is also known to cause breast cancer and mental disorders, as well as alter the behavior of certain insect species.

Soiled Stargazing

Light pollution can affect the experience of recreational activities, which includes stargazing. More artificial light causes celestial objects to be less visible in the night sky.
